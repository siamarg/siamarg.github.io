INDUSTRIAL AND COMMERCIAL PACKING

As an alternative to traditional plastics based on fossil fuel, bioplastics are widely used in food contacts (FCMs). For e.g., The company Coca Cola recently released its plant bottle, partly made of Bioorganic plastics, and for its yoghurt cups Danone uses polyactide (PLA). There are two distinct kinds of bioplastics: biobased polymers and biodegradable plastics. Oxo-biodegradable plastics and bio-nanocomposites are two more basic concepts for biorelated plastics.


Biobased polymers are produced from biobased materials, but their quality in practice can vary. The biomass used in bioplastics can either be harvested directly (starch, cellulose) from plants or generated by fermentation micro-organisms (e.g. Polyhydroxyalcanoates (PHA)). Biomass can be from feedstocks of the first generation (e.g. Maize, sugar cane) or non-food crops (e.g. Lignocellulosic content of the second generation). Other chemical changes can also create bio-based polymers which are not inherently biodegradable.


Bioplastics from the 1990s were traditionally considered to be biodegradable. Although the question of the end-of - life management of plastic waste products was just taking shape, the topic became more and more significant. The raw materials on the market (mainly starch- based compounds and PLA) were therefore specifically developed for disposable applications where biodegradable and compostable polymers were usefully employed. For this purpose, the markets for versatile packaging (film, bags, etc.) And rigid packages were initially combined (packaging tray, bottles, glasses, etc.).


The diversification of the applications of bioplastics began with the arrival over the years of new materials and consolidated all plastics engineering markets gradually, assisted with development in R&D that allowed properties to be improved.


Today, rigid packaging and consumer products, which mainly include plant-based PET and PUR, dominate the bio plastic industry.


There is a major separation of the various uses for bioplastics, while biodegradable and compostable polymers are primarily used in sectors in which this property is technically beneficial before or after their use, such as farming, horticulture and versatile packaging (biodegradable bags). In comparison, they are virtually absent from the transportation and manufacturing industries.

AVAILABLE PRODUCTS

LAMINATION FILMS

Siamarg Bio Film can be laminated with a paper tray which can be substituted for envelope windows or other food packaging. This material is created by burying or manure and can be fully decomposed into water and carbon dioxide. It has no toxins and has no harmful effects on the climate. Biodegradable Composable is a fresh and high polymer substance made from plant extract (monomer substance) manufactured through chemical synthesis; it contains non-toxic and non-irritating as its characteristics and can be buried in soil.

SHRINK FILMS

Bio-polymer-based shrink films consisting of Siamarg Bio Products are made of maize compounds using plant starch. Siamarg is a biodegradable alternative to plant shrink film. These products are made of bio-polymers and are the earth-friendly plastics of the new future that is emerging.

TEXTILE PACKAGING

Siamarg Bio Products offers packaging options for the clothing industry. Our goods are 100% biodegradable, compostable and are made from organic raw materials. Our Clothing, Bags have no heavy metals. They contribute strongly in elimination of synthetic plastic used by Multinational Clothing Brands and to a neutralize carbon footprint.

FOOD PACKING

Polysaccharides are common macromolecules for the manufacturing of food packaging. And if the packaging is recycled by the user, it is biodegradable. Cellulose, chitin, and Pullulan are common polysaccharides. The positive thing about these polymers is that they have a good mechanical ability. They are also capable of isolating the substance from oxygen and carbon dioxide. But these polymers are hydrophilic, i.e. They do not give a moisture barrier.
